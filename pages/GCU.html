About Government College University

The Government College University, Lahore (colloquially known as GCU) is a public research university located in Lahore, Pakistan.
Established in 1861 as the Government College, it opened its doors in 1864.After being elevated to a university status in 2002.
It has advanced into one of the top ten largest institution in the Pakistan with student body of over 12,000. GCU has 32 academic departments, segregated into five faculties.
There are five research centers, focused on academic and industrial research and development projects.It has the highest graduation rate in the country, with an average of 95.5% annually.
The university secured its second place in the general category by the Higher Education Commission (Pakistan) (HEC) in 2013.
GCU is noted for its historical roots and attracted notable scholars such as poet-philosopher Allama Muhammad Iqbal, Nobel laureates Har Gobind Khorana and Abdus Salam.Few more disciplines will be added in near future.
